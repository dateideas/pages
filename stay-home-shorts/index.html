Singapore Art Museum - Stay Home Shorts

For a limited time period only, these short films will be available from their scheduled times up till Sunday, 21 June 2020, 11.59pm

Singapore Art Museum presents 'Stay Home Shorts', an online film series featuring local and international short films.

Starting in June 2020, the series kicks off with a special selection of four films from previous editions of SAM’s signature children’s art exhibition Art Garden. These light-hearted films centre around hope, and address the importance of family, relationships, and communication.

Parental guidance is advised

Visit SAM's youtube channel to watch these clips at their scheduled timings compiled by our team just for you!

Dear Fatty

by Hsin-I Tseng | in English with English and Chinese subtitles | 2008 | 7min

A young girl writes a letter to her runaway pet hamster, Fatty.

Scheduled for 19 June 2020, Noon

Chat

by Tan Siang Yu | in English with English subtitles | 2012 | 10min 43 sec

Robert is a typical teenager whose mobile phone is an extension of his body. One day he accidentally loses this “vital organ.” This personal crisis leads him on a little journey of self-discovery where he learns the true art of communication.

Scheduled for 19 June 2020, 8pm

Hush Baby

by Tan Wei Keong | No dialogue | 2009 | 3min 40sec

A curious baby plays and struggles in a confined and restrictive environment.

Scheduled for 20 June 2020, Noon

Family Dinner

by Clara Gan & Shen Jiahui | No dialogue | 2012 | 4min 18sec

A stay-home bunny dad is dissatisfied with his family life and attempts to cook a sumptuous family dinner. However, the dinner slowly falls apart as it unfolds.

Scheduled for 20 June 2020, 8pm
